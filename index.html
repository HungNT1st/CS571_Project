Understanding FDI & PAPI

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is when a foreign company invests in a country's businesses or assets, often by establishing operations or acquiring local firms. It plays a vital role in economic growth by creating jobs, boosting productivity, and introducing new technologies. Looking at FDI by province helps highlight regional strengths and uncover areas that need more support. This information guides policymakers in attracting investment and promoting balanced development across the country.


The Provincial Governance and Public Administration Performance Index (PAPI) measures the quality of governance and public service delivery across provinces based on citizen surveys. It covers areas like transparency, accountability, public participation, and control of corruption. Tracking PAPI helps identify strengths and weaknesses in local governance, guiding reforms and policy improvements. Comparing scores across provinces encourages healthy competition and supports more responsive, citizen-centered governance.

Key Insights

This interactive dashboard provides a comprehensive view of Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) across Vietnam's provinces over time and its relationship with governance quality, measured by the Provincial Governance and Public Administration Performance Index (PAPI). It helps users explore how regional investment trends align with governance performance across multiple dimensions.

  • FDI varies significantly by province, with some regions showing negative inflows.
  • National FDI peaked in 2023 at over $70 billion, then dropped to $29 billion in 2024.
  • Most PAPI dimensions show little to no correlation with FDI.
